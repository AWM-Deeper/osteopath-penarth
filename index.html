About Your Practitioner

David Langdon
BSc(Hons) Osteopathy

David moved back to Wales in 2019 after living in London for the last 30 years & is working in Penarth from Old Penarthians RFC, & at The Vale Foot Clinic in the High Street, Barry. After spending 20 years as a Medical Photographer working in a number of hospitals around the UK & after 10 years working at King’s College Hospital he decided to retrain as an Osteopath at the London School of Osteopathy. On graduating in 2008 with a BSc (Hons) in Osteopathy, he opened his own clinic & gym, Langdon Osteopathy, near London’s Canary Wharf which he ran until deciding to move to Penarth. He is also qualified as an Active Release Technique (Spine) therapist, a western acupuncture practitioner, is qualified in the use of Kinesio Taping, food intolerance testing, & is a Poliquin Certified Personal Trainer & Kinetic Link Training (KLT) instructor.

David’s main interest outside of osteopathy is sport, especially rugby union. He played rugby at London Welsh from 1992 to 2010 retiring through injury at the grand age of 44. David has kept in touch with rugby by working for 9 seasons as Head of Therapy at Old Priorian RFC & 4 seasons as Head of Therapy for London Welsh RFC where he provided osteopathic/rehab services & match day pitch-side 1st aid cover for the men & womens teams. He now works with Old Penarthians RFC as their Head of Therapy providing the same service.

It is his interest in sport and his personal experience of recovering from a number of injuries that brought him to osteopathy and he’s keen to build on his experience of treating and rehabilitating sports men and women competing at all levels from amateurs to professionals. It is important to remember however that it isn’t David’s intention to only treat sports people but also to help anyone who may need his services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Osteopathy is a form of manual therapy that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of musculoskeletal disorders. Osteopaths use hands-on techniques including stretching, massage and manipulation to improve joint mobility, relieve muscle tension and enhance blood supply to tissues.
